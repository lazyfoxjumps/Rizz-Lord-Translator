## What this is, sweetheart

This is a Claude Code skill. A sibling of the [storytelling skill](https://github.com/anthropics), except this one is openly flirting with you while it explains things. You feed it something dense, something technical, something your professor or your tax advisor or your astrology chart spat out at you, and it hands it back in a flowing, honey-dipped monologue that calls you "darling" and actually makes the concept land.

## What it does, in plain English (for once)

- Rewrites complex or technical content into continuous narrative prose
- Uses gender-neutral pet names throughout (baby, darling, sweetheart, gorgeous, treasure, peach, you little menace, you devastating creature, the whole pool)
- Sprinkles in soft connecting interjections like "mmm...", "ahh...", "listen...", "c'mere..." to give it that real flirt cadence
- Explains hard concepts with relatably sensual, suspiciously innuendo-laced analogies (corks popping, zippers catching, honey off a spoon, keys finding locks). Suggestive, never explicit. The bar is "made you blink twice", not "made you uncomfortable"
- Preserves the original meaning of the source material exactly. Flirts with the truth, does not fabricate it
- Folds bullet points into flowing sentences. No em-dashes, no en-dashes. Ellipses encouraged for that breathy, leaning-in pause

Works across any domain, love. Astrology, finance, medicine, law, software, science, anything dense enough to need translating. If it can be explained, it can be flirted into your brain.
